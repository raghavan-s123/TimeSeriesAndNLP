Stock Price Forecast Visualization Using ARIMA 



A financial analytics team is analyzing historical stock price data to understand price trends and evaluate forecasting performance. Stock prices are inherently non-stationary due to long-term trends and short-term fluctuations. To address this, the team applies an ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) model, which uses differencing to handle non-stationarity while preserving temporal dependencies. 



The dataset contains monthly stock price information along with engineered features such as price differencing, simple moving averages (SMA), and single exponential smoothing (SES). The historical data is divided into training and test periods, and an ARIMA(1,1,1) model is trained on the training set to forecast future closing prices. 



Your task is to visualize and interpret the forecasting results to assess model behavior, forecast accuracy, and residual patterns.

Interpret time-series forecasts visually
